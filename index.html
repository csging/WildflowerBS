We work in partnership with you, providing everything you need from routine preventitve care to the most serious medical intervention. We pride ourselves in using the most up-to-date information, diagnostic tools, and interventions to provide the least invasive care allowing you the quickest, most comfortable recovery.

Services Provided

In Office

Well Exam
This preventative exam is usually done yearly to access vital information such as weight, blood pressure and height. An exam is done to access thyroid, lungs, heart, breast, abdomen and pelvis looking for signs of problems and addressing them.
PAP Smear
Cells are scraped from the cervix and sent to a lab to detect cervical cancer and pre-cancer.
Colposcopy
Viewing the cervix with magnification to perform biopsies, looking for precancerous cells.
LEEP
The use of a wire loop with current to remove abnormal areas of the cervix and prevent cancer.
Endometrial Biopsy
Sampling of the tissue that lines the uterine cavity most often done to look for the cause of abnormal bleeding and help exclude cancer.
Hysteroscopy with Dialation and Curettage
Using a small scope to look inside the uterus and sample the lining to look for infection, polyps, cancer and other pathology.
Ultrasound
A noninvasive method of visualizing pelvic organs to access for masses, cysts and other abnormalities. Also, can detect early pregnancies inside and outside of the uterus. Used in obstetrics to access the baby inside the uter
Long Acting Contraceptives
Implantation of devices for birth control tha are removable. These may contain hormones but are safer than birth control pills containing estrogen.
Hormone replacement Therapy
Replacing the hormones that are no longer produced after a woman goes through menopause. This is done to reduce symptoms. Breast and uterus cancer risks may be increased with their use, but they can usually be used safely in small amount with great benefits and better quality of life.

Hospital Procedures

Minimally Invasive Surgery
Laparoscopic (with a small scope) or vaginal procedures that avoid large incisions and more painful recovery and scar formation. These procedures allow diagnosis of gynecologic problems and surgical removal of organs, cysts, scar tissue and biopsies of tissue. These include: Diagnostic Laparoscopy, Operative Laparoscopy, Tubal Ligation, Cystectomy, Hysterectomy.
Sterilization
Removal or clamping of the Fallopian tubes to prevent pregnancy. This may be done through a small scope and does not require a hospital stay. Also called Tubal Ligation.
Cystectomy
Removal and most often testing of a cyst (fluid filled structure) usually found on the ovaries. Most of ovarian cysts are benign and do not require removal. Usually done through tiny incisions as an outpatient.
Myomectomy
Removal of a benign muscle and fibrous growths from the uterus, either through a scope or abdominal incision.
Hysterectomy
Removal of the uterus. The tubes and ovaries may be removed at the same time depending on the circumstances. Often the tubes are removed at the time of the hysterectomy as they have been found to be a source of cancer and they have no function after child bearing. Ovaries may be retained if the patient desires them for hormones and if they appear normal. Hysterectomies are done through minimally invasive techniques unless the uterus is too large or there are other masses or complications requiring a larger incision. The preferred method, if possible, is to remove the uterus vaginally. Vaginal hysterectomy has been shown to have the lowest complication rate.
